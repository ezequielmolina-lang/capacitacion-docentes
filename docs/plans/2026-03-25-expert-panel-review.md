# Expert Panel Review — Capacitación Docente Virtual
## Eligiendo Mi Camino | 25 de marzo de 2026

Based on CDR framework (Making Teacher Policy Work, Ch.2), in-service TPD best practices (Ch.4), and review of the training content against source documents (Guía del Docente de Matemáticas, Guía del Tutor OV).

---

## CDR FRAMEWORK ANALYSIS

### Target Changes Expected of Teachers
1. Use the AI tutoring platform (uDocz) 2x/week in lab sessions
2. Monitor student progress via dashboard — NOT intervene during AI practice
3. Plan semester integrating lab + classroom sessions (4h vs 6h schools)
4. Follow the SOMA/5-moment session architecture
5. Manage logistics: registration, passwords, lab scheduling, parent communication
6. Join and participate in WhatsApp support network

### Is It CLEAR? — Rating: 3/5
**What works:** Platform description is solid, Doc's Socratic method well explained, Modo Escuela vs Casa clear.
**Gaps:**
- SOMA/5-moment model mentioned but not broken down into specific teacher actions
- B vs B' experimental design barely explained — teachers don't know which arm their school is in
- Dashboard interpretation described but not practiced
- "Don't give answers during frustration" is said but never demonstrated
- Teacher's exact role during lab sessions not articulated minute-by-minute

### Is It DOABLE? — Rating: 2.5/5
**What works:** Platform exploration (hands-on with uDocz), planning with AI, checklist steps.
**Gaps:**
- ~70% passive content (reading/watching) vs ~30% active (quizzes/practice) — should be closer to 50/50
- No session simulation — teacher never practices running a 40-min lab session
- No dashboard practice with realistic data
- No troubleshooting scenarios (internet down, student can't login, copying, crying student)
- Planning module generates a plan but doesn't practice executing it
- Ch.4 evidence: "passive training approaches made it difficult for teachers to effectively implement" — applies here

### Is It REWARDING? — Rating: 2.5/5
**What works:** Diploma, WhatsApp community link, friendly conversational tone.
**Gaps:**
- No peer success stories ("Profe María tried this and her students loved it")
- No connection to teacher identity ("You're not being replaced — you're being upgraded")
- No quick wins showing student engagement data
- No recognition of the difficulty of change
- Ch.2 evidence: "making the activity social for teachers" increases adoption — we should leverage the WhatsApp community more

## EXPERT 1: LEARNING SCIENCE (Retrieval, Spacing, Cognitive Load)

| Dimension | Rating | Assessment |
|-----------|--------|------------|
| Retrieval Practice | 3/5 | Quizzes exist after content blocks — good. But no retrieval between modules. No "Do you remember from Module 1?" at start of Module 2. |
| Spacing | 2/5 | Banner says "1 module per day recommended" but removed. No structural spacing incentives. Could lock modules for 24h (rejected by user — keep free). At minimum, show "Tip: come back tomorrow for better retention." |
| Cognitive Load | 3/5 | Content steps vary widely — some are very dense (Modo Escuela vs Casa + 3 modes + diagnostic questions all in one step). Should chunk more. |
| Active Learning | 2.5/5 | ~30% active. Needs more. Every content step should be followed by a doing step. |
| Transfer | 2/5 | Platform exploration helps, but no classroom simulation. The gap between "I understand the platform" and "I can run a lab session" is not bridged. |
| Feedback Quality | 4/5 | Quiz feedback is diagnostic — explains WHY, not just right/wrong. This is a strength. |
| Modeling | 2/5 | Training teaches Socratic method but doesn't USE Socratic method. Teaches retrieval practice but barely uses it. Should practice what it preaches. |

**Top Recommendation:** Add retrieval practice quizzes at the START of each module (not just end). Add a session simulation exercise. Increase active:passive ratio.
